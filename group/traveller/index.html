Comment by Chris P on October 30, 2009 at 5:37am
Jake, you're instincts are correct in this case. One of the few criticisms that can be made for the Traveller universe (...whatever system you happen to use) is the sheer size and scale of it. It's very easy for GM's and players to be overwhelmed with information before the first adventure of the first campaign gets off the ground...so to speak.

The show Firefly provides some really good ideas on how to present and run a basic Traveller adventure: the characters are each simple in structure, but complex in back story (thus, making them interesting for players) while the objectives of each episode is fairly straightforward and can be boiled down to one sentence (e.g. "Do the job and get paid", "Take cattle from planet A to planet B and get paid", "Get the part to fix the ship" etc). The rest is just detail and can be added whenever you (...and your players) feel more comfortable with the universe as a whole.

Hope this helps...
