First matter of discussion: What is one of your favorite genres for a setting that you think has been severely forgotten?

I'll give you an example: Time Travel Science Fiction. It's a hard setting to do well, and so we haven't seen many examples of it.

Permalink Reply by Pseudoephedrine on October 27, 2009 at 8:20am
I don't think there are a lot of good late antiquity/dark ages settings, or good historically accurate Asian settings (esp. India, but SE Asia is also severely underrepresented). In general, I would say that, despite being some of the most interesting periods and places in history, transitional periods and culturally-mixed locations are extremely under-represented despite being perfect for adventuring. Some examples include the 5th to 7th century AD in western Europe, the Greco-Bactrian kingdoms in central Asia, Central America during the 16th century and I'm sure better read individuals than me could think of others.
