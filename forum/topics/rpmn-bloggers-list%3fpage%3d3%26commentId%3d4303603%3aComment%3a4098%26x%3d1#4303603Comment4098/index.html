Since the number of members is growing very rapidly and everyone is still in the stage of getting to know each other. I thought it would be a great idea to have a few threads that list down the URL links to our respective media.

So here I'll compile the list of blogs that everyone owns in this network so we can easily reference or search each other in the future. Just reply to this thread with;

1)name of blog
2)URL of blog
3)A short blurb (maybe about a paragraph) of what you want to say about your blog

What do you say? I'll go first.

Questing GM; A Borneon Gamer
questinggm.blogspot.com
The Questing GM hails from a small gaming tribe hidden in the mountains and jungles of Borneo. He is on a quest to search for the long lost secrets of gaming Enlightenment. Join him in his quest to discuss and learn more about gaming, GMing, news and the minority views about gaming in the east.

Scintilla RPG Design
scintillarpg.wordpress.com
Scintilla is my blog for game design. Currently, two projects are in the works, with occasional updates on the blog. One is a dark and grim fantasy setting called Ethereal. The other is high adventure fantasy, currently called Turmoil. Turmoil takes place in the distant past of Ethereal, and supplies many of the myths and legends that populate Ethereal's setting.

Nuketown (blog)
http://www.nuketown.com

Nuketown Radio Active (podcast)
http://www.nuketown.com

My name's Ken Newquist and I've been publishing Nuketown in one form or another since 1996. It features a mix of geek-related content (movies, books, video games, comics, etc.) but has always had a hefty amount of role-playing game content. Most of the time I'm blogging about lessons learned from my current campaign; historically that was set in the World of Greyhawk (http://wiki.griffcrier.com) but for the last year it's been my Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic game.

That said, I try and stay away from "let me tell you about my character" posts and delve more into what makes the game tick, what works (and what doesn't) and how the heck I can keep doing this when I've got two kids under age six. :)
