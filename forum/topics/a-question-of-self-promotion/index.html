A Question of Self Promotion

  • Posted by Matthew Swetnam on January 4, 2010 at 3:44pm in General Discussion
  • View Discussions
I've been working on my own RPG system and setting for some time now. I'm to a point where I need it to be playtested to work out the kinks. However, I have a hard time promoting myself or my game. Any time I start to mention it I stop short, thinking I don't want to bother or annoy anyone.

Has anyone else experienced this? Better yet, are there any suggestions on how to get past it?

Permalink Reply by WJ MacGuffin on January 5, 2010 at 11:16am
That's what teh internet is for! Post requests for playtesters on forge, rpg.net, and the like. That way, you're not really bothering anyone since they have to reply to get the stuff. However, if you plan on printing and selling the product, you might want these anonymous playtesters to sign a legal doc saying they won't steal your work and pass it off as their own.

Another idea is to run the playtests yourself at cons. I've done that for two years now for my upcoming game, and it's been more helpful that I have words to explain. Even if you can't go to cons, you could play online through Skype or rpg software.

Lastly, I'd be happy to take a look at it. Between my job and my toddler, I don't game much so I can't playtest it, but I can at least critique the system. wjmacguffin (at) gmail (dot) com.

Permalink Reply by F. Douglas Wall 1 day ago
I'm actually pretty introverted myself, so this is something that I've had to deal with as well.

The first thing I had to realize is that if I wanted to see something done, I was going to have to be the one to do it. No one is going to go out of their way to help you unless you ask them. It may take time to work up the determination to do something, but you'd be surprised how many people won't be annoyed by your requests.

Also, make sure that everyone knows that you're a human being, not an ad-bot. When I post on forums and such, I make sure to A) Mention my game (Adventures in Oz, BTW) in contexts where it is appropriate (threads for "What's a good game to start my kids on?" and the like) and B) try to contribute to other discussions as well, so that I can get comfortable with the forum and the other posters can get comfortable with me.
