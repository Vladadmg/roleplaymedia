Publicity question for bloggers, podcasters

  • Posted by WJ MacGuffin on December 11, 2009 at 8:56am in General Discussion
  • View Discussions
Hi folks! I have a question for all of you bloggers, podcasters, and similar rpg media types. How should I handle asking for publicity?

For example, I'm hard at work on a new rpg called Triune. I obviously want some media exposure to build some buzz prior to release. Is this something y'all are interested in? Yes, I know some blogs have narrower topics and aren't appropriate for getting the news out. (No reason to bother a blogger who writes about Lords & Labyrinths with my new game.)

What about the others? If you run a rpg news or general site, would you prefer an email with a traditional press release? Would you prefer not to be contacted because, if the game is buzzworthy and therefore blogworthy, it will already have some buzz? Is anyone interested in interviewing or does that sound too egotistical?

Lastly, since this is a media network, could we create a list of bloggers and podcasters interested in small press news and updates?

Permalink Reply by Michael Wolf on December 15, 2009 at 2:54pm
Hi WJ! I don't think it's that a bad idea to try to contact some bloggers you know first. Of course someone who focuses on D&D 4E or Lords & Labyrinth wouldn't be that interested but perhaps he (or she) knows someone else who might be. The easiest way to get attention is by a) offering bloggers to get free review copies and b) create a free quick-start PDF to show off your game. Sending out press releases might work, too, but I never have got any press releases (aside from the one on newsletters I already subscribed to), so I can't really comment on them.
The best way to get people interested is probably contacting them directly.
And I think creating a list of bloggers and podcasters interested in small press is a great idea. This could probably help indie/small press publishers/designers and bloggers/podcasters alike. Go ahead and feel free to add me to this list!

P.S.: I will have a look at your site and I will think about your interview offer.
