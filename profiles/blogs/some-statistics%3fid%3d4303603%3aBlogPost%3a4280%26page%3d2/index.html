Some statistics

  • Posted by Michael Wolf on October 28, 2009 at 4:00am
  • View Michael Wolf's blog
I used the advanced search function of the Role Play Media Network to retrieve some information about our current members. I have to admit I was quite intrigued about what I found out. Perhaps this might be of interest to some of you too.

Total members
As of 10:52 GMT+1 on October 28th we have 289 members. Wow! The official announcement was just a couple days ago, so this is quite an achievement. And it seems the clichés are all correct: gamers are predominantly male. 253 members are male, 17 female and obviously 19 members chose not to disclose this information. Or the advanced search function is faulty.

Players and GMs
According to our database we currently have 214 members who are players and 228 gamemasters. Wow. That not only means that there are more GMs than players on this network (which is enforcing the alpha gamer theory), it also shows that NOT everyone here plays RPGs (never or just at the moment). It's also possible that people didn't want to disclose this information. But if you ask me this totally defeats the purpose of joining a social network like this one.

Bloggers, Podcasters, Game Designers and more
The biggest group following the players and GMs are bloggers. 185 members (that are 64%) are RPG bloggers. This is not really that surprising since the community assembled here probably around the RPG Bloggers Network and it's members and readers. 164 members are doing writing of some sorts, 130 do some game design. But only 47 members have listed art as one of the types of media they are involved in. 45 members do some cartography.
It's interesting that only 38 people are doing podcasts. That are 13% and much less than I anticipated. Only 19 people are involved with videos.

The network is still brand-new but the structure of our membership is more or less like I anticipated. Our community is mostly made up by male bloggers (or at least gamemasters). What baffles me is the fact that there are a few people who are neither players nor GMs. We should make sure to get them playing in no time! I also would like to bring more players (who are not bloggers or GMs) to the network.

What are your thoughts on that matter? And what conclusions do you draw from those statistics?
